Cybersecurity is the art and science of staying safe online..

Cybersecurity is also described as the protection of data and computer systems from information disclosure, theft or damage.

The information superage started in the mid 1990s. Since then, our communication, our finances, our medical records, and our personal connections have had a digital life. Data can be compromised, it is exposed on the .

Criminals want to gain access to our digital data, and want to compromise that data. Cybersecurity is concerned with

  • keeping userids and passwords safe
  • disguising data sent over the internet
  • keeping malware (software intended to cause harm) away from our computers

Cybercrime can bring software systems to a halt, because everything in the modern world is directed by software. Cybercriminals can take over a hospital, a gas pipeline, a school or a personal computer. Even if you don;t experience a direct attack, you can be indirectly impacted by cybercrime.

Digital data flows through the wires of the internet. It holds financial information, personal information, medical information. It is accessible, by those who can use it for negative purposes, and these individuals are called cybercriminals. This data should be protected, it should be secured as it is stored or transmitted, and kept safe from criminals who rely on system and data vulnerabilities. This is what cybersecurity is all about.
